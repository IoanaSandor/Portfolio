Type: Animation Mentor Assignment

Role: Ideation, Planning, Animating, Rendering

Tools: Maya, Photoshop, Premiere Pro

|

In this assignment, I was tasked with demonstrating intermediate body mechanics. I really wanted to challenge myself and try animating with a different rig so I chose Stan. This also presented the opportunity to have some fun with the acting of the shot.

process

The first thing I did was plan and film reference. As my workflow has developed, I've taken a liking to planning directly on my reference material with the help of programs like SyncSketch. In my reference, I tried to be as authentic to my animation mechanics wise as possible by carrying a light object as the chair, making sure it was much smaller than my body, and by wearing proper shoes so the steps would feel authentic. I also set up two camera angles and aligned the footage in order to get multiple angles for reference.


After my basic planning was done, I began roughly blocking my key poses and committing to a shot cam very early in the process.


Once my rough blocking was done and approved by my mentor, I polished my blocking and made sure to define my breakdowns in preparation for moving from stepped to spline.


After I polished my blocking, I did an very rough pass in spline. The animation was in very rough shape at this point and I had a lot of work ahead of me.


I started cleaning up my curves and getting a better sense of my timing as I kept refining. At this point, I started adding small flourishes that added personality like the scrunched facial expression and the approving nod.


At this stage, I added more polished gestures like the way Stan let go of the chair after initially setting it down and the delicate way he adjusts it. This is also when I really started cleaning up my arcs and added a simple animation on the squirrel as well.


final thoughts

Although I see so many areas in which more polish could be added, I am happy with this animation. I really enjoyed the challenges of posing a character with such odd proportions. It felt so satisfying when I solved issues pertaining to his large hands, small head, and tiny feet. Furthermore, by giving this character a strong personality and intention from the beginning, it really made acting choices and details a lot of fun and purposeful. All in all, I believe that this was a great start to a mechanics shot!
