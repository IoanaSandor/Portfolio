Type: Animation Mentor Assignment

Role: Ideation, Planning, Animating, Rendering

Tools: Maya, Premiere Pro

|

I was tasked with animating a walk cycle. At first I wanted to do something a little crazier, but my mentor suggested that starting with a basic vanilla walk might be better for building a strong foundation in mechanics.

process

First, I recorded some video reference that I used to help me plan out my animation. I used SyncSketch in order to create notes and annotations on my video directly.


Then, I started blocking out my key poses and breakdowns from 3 different angles to make sure everything was working properly. We needed to animate two full cycles so I set my curves to repeat once I finished a full cycle.


Next, I moved into spline. I wanted to focus on the legs and the hips so I hid the arms. During this process I encountered knee pops and could not figure out why my steps felt so soft.


After that was done, I added the arms back in a also polished them.


At this polish stage I realised why my knee pops were happening and how to fix the soft-ness of my steps. I needed to take less frames to bring the foot down after the contact pose and also that the knee pops were caused by too much variation in height.


final thoughts

I am happy with the final product. I think I learned a lot about all the different moving parts in a walk and how the weight shifts from not only side to side, but also up and down.
